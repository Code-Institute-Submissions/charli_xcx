How I'm Feeling Now:

The British electro pop artist sings of love, loneliness, and solo partying across an album built in and made for this very moment. - Pitchfork

The ‘workaholic party animal’ has, under the glare of social media, put together the ultimate lockdown album. - The Guardian

Charli XCX, How I’m Feeling Now: ultra-plastic pop becomes cutting-edge sonic art. - The Telegraph

Charli:

Charli XCX, With Friends, Maps Pop’s Future on ‘Charli’. Her first official LP in five years gathers a fluid global dream team for a wild, joyously bummed-out set of avant-pop bangers. - Rolling Stone

With adventurous production and revealing writing, Charli XCX’s third studio album reflects an artist ready to commit to self-examination. - Pitchfork

Returning to the studio with PC Music’s A.G. Cook, Charli XCX's new album is boundlessly liberating, decadently indulgent, and irresistibly danceable. - The Skinny

Number 1 Angel:

Number 1 Angel is a maybe mixtape, sorta free, but released by a voice that’s constantly solving life’s real problems with the imagined solutions of pop music. “I’m a dreamer,” Charli reminds us. Pop is dream stuff for bodies. - Tiny Mixtapes

Charli XCX Commits to Avant-Pop Partnership on ‘Number 1 Angel’. - Rolling Stone

With Number 1 Angel, Charli XCX gives us a preview of what’s to come, not just for her album, but all of pop music. - Pretty Much Amzing

Pop2:

It’s hard to think of another recent pop album that writhes, squirms and splinters like this one. - Spectrum Culture

Charli XCX’s latest mixtape is a vision of what pop music could be, the sound of an eclectic, hyperreal future where romantic love is fun but fucked and partying is an emotional refuge. - Pitchfork

The English singer-songwriter offers up irresistible artifacts in strange, beautiful places. - Consequence of Sound

True Romance:

It's not surprising that this is a strong debut, but just how consistently catchy and personal True Romance is might raise a few eyebrows. - All Music

After some big early singles and some unsteady mixtapes, Charlotte Aitchison's debut album delievers on that initial promise. True Romance is an album of emotionally direct, bubblegum-catchy, off-kilter songs about falling in and out of love. - Pitchfork

Raised in semirural Hertfordshire, England, Aitchison got her performance legs by spitting verses at raves, and fittingly, she’s a Jedi master of potent motifs - Rolling Stone

Sucker:

After years of being a a critical darling Charli XCX assisted Iggy Azalea on the song of the summer and landed her own "Boom Clap" on the soundtrack for The Fault in Our Stars. Sucker is her second studio album, and it's a record that seeks to bend the mainstream to its will rather than conform to its reigning trends. - Pitchfork

This year’s first great pop record bowls in with a rapturous celebration of the genre's rebellious, trashy potential (and a bottle of champagne and a pocketful of pills to boot). - NME

Thanks to "Fancy" and "Boom Clap," Charli's voice is already among the most ubiquitous of the year. But Sucker is likely to confirm that she's one of pop's biggest new stars -- and biggest personalities. - Billboard
